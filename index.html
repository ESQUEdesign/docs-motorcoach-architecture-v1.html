Section I

Site Architecture

From a flat 20-page sprawl with six overlapping inventory surfaces to a consolidated four-pillar dealership ecosystem

Design principle: The Motorcoach Store is the luxury end of the RV market — Prevost, Newell, Millennium, LOKI. The site should read like a private showroom, not a classified-ads lot. Every coach earns an editorial presentation; every service earns a single authoritative URL. Navigation labels are destinations ("Coaches," "Sell or Consign," "Service"), not marketing verbs.

Current-State Audit — crawl-surface findings

  • Inventory fragmentation (cannibalization): six overlapping inventory surfaces compete for the same queries — /coach/, /new-coaches/, /pre-owned/, /certified-pre-owned/, /pre-owned-showrooms/, /explore-showrooms/. Link equity and keyword targeting are split six ways.
  • Root-level listing slugs: vehicle detail pages live at the domain root (e.g. /2010-prevost-emerald-outlaw/). No hierarchy → no breadcrumbs, no inventory-hub equity flow, and sold units strand 404s at root.
  • Inconsistent brand-showroom patterns: /motorcoach-showroom-ogv/ vs /exclusive-loki-coach-dealer/ — two naming systems for the same content type.
  • Duplicate contact surfaces: /contact-us/ and /contact-us-auction/ split the conversion path; auction should be a routed tab, not a second page.
  • Blog at /news/ exists but is thin; no AEO-structured answer content anywhere on the domain.

Anchor sections (§), not separate pages: Sub-topics (cash offer, consignment, trade-in, auction, maintenance) are scroll-anchored sections within their parent hub, not standalone URLs. This consolidates link equity into the pillar URLs, prevents thin-content pages, and keeps the crawl surface tight. Pages that do earn dedicated URLs: the pillars, the inventory child lists (new / pre-owned / certified), the CPO & PDI program pages, brand showrooms, the distinct service pages (Paint & Body, Ceramic Coating — per the ClearBox reconciliation each is a distinct keyword + FAQ surface), and individual vehicle pages — vehicle pages because each unit is a unique, high-intent transactional document; brand pages because "Prevost for sale," "Newell for sale," and "LOKI Coach dealer" are distinct high-value query families.

Universal Inquire CTA pattern, routed by content area: Every pillar ends in a single shared conversion module routed to a different team via a hidden recipient_team form field set by the page's content-area property: Coaches → sales team (with "Schedule a Showroom Visit" intent option, vehicle pages inject the unit's stock # and title), Sell or Consign → acquisition/auction team (valuation form: year, make, model, mileage, photos), Service → service writers (links to HubSpot Meetings scheduler), Home/About/Contact → general sales inbox. One form schema, one design, four routed pipelines.

Section I ⇄ ClearBox Proposal — Reconciliation

Reconciled ✓ — 2026-06-13 Diffed against the ClearBox proposal (motorcoach-clearbox-proposal.vercel.app, retrieved with the client visitor password). The proposal is a 384-hour, 4-week HubSpot rebuild scoped at 58 inventory units (48 active + 10 sold), 67 inventory pages, a 30-URL sitemap, and 93% live-verified facts. Its architecture is an optimize-in-place plan; Section I is a more aggressive consolidate-and-restructure plan. They agree on diagnosis and differ on how far to restructure URLs and navigation.

  • Inventory spans $93K–$2.15M across Prevost, Newell, Newmar, Entegra, Tiffin, OGV, ShowHauler, Featherlite; exclusive U.S. dealer for Prevost LOKI Coach.
  • IA additions the v1.0 audit missed, now folded in: Ceramic Coating and Paint & Body as distinct service pages; PDI as a program page; RV Insurance and Trade-In as supporting pages; ShowHauler & Featherlite as new-coach lines.
  • Confirmed staging cruft to noindex/remove (not real destinations): /testin-hero, /old_home_page_may-2026, /dev, /contact-us-auction/.

Section XV

SEO & AEO Strategy

Traditional search + answer-engine optimization, built into the architecture from day one

Strategic context: Aggregators (RV Trader, RVT.com, Autotrader RV) currently outrank the dealer for its own inventory queries, and AI engines answer "where to buy a used Prevost" from those aggregators. The consolidation in Section I plus the schema and answer-content program below puts TMCS's own voice in front of both crawlers and answer engines — no luxury-coach competitor is investing in AEO yet.

SEO Architecture Principles

1 · One H1 per page, always descriptive. "Pre-Owned Luxury Motorcoaches for Sale" — never a slogan. Vehicle pages: "[Year] [Make] [Model] for Sale."

2 · Canonical URLs & full 301 map. Every legacy URL redirects per Section I. Filter facets canonicalize to /coaches. Sold units stay live in "Sold" state. No root-level listing slugs survive.

3 · Schema markup, 8 types. AutoDealer (LocalBusiness) sitewide w/ NAP, hours, geo; Vehicle + Offer on every unit; ItemList on inventory hubs; BreadcrumbList everywhere; FAQPage on /financing, /sell, CPO; VideoObject on walkthroughs; Person (owner) on /about; Article on /news. Current site: minimal/no structured data.

4 · Internal linking mesh. Brand pages ↔ filtered inventory ↔ vehicle pages ↔ similar units; /sell and /financing cross-linked from every vehicle page; breadcrumbs everywhere.

5 · Core Web Vitals. LCP <2.5s (preloaded hero, WebP/AVIF via HubSpot CDN), CLS <0.1 (reserved gallery dimensions), INP <200ms (deferred GTM/chat). Gallery-heavy vehicle pages get strict lazy-loading below the first viewport.
